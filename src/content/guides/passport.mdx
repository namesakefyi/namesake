**Update November 6, 2025:**

The Supreme Court has allowed the Trump administration to [enforce its discriminatory passport policy](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/supreme-court-allows-trump-administration-to-enforce-discriminatory-passport-policy). **Changes to gender markers on passports currently run the risk of being denied.**

For more information, the ACLU has published a [Q&A guide about Orr v. Trump](https://www.aclu.org/trumps-assault-on-transgender-rights/qa-orr-v-trump).

## Get prepared

To change your name on your passport, you will need a completed **court order **and a new **social security** **card** reflecting your new name.

## Fill out forms

You can use the official passport filling tool here:

[Fill out forms](https://pptform.state.gov/)

## Print and assemble documents

Print forms single-sided. The state department will not accept double-sided forms.

Get a new 2×2" passport photo. You can do this yourself, although we recommend getting yours professionally done at a local post office or pharmacy to ensure it’s compliant (they can be picky!).

Assemble your documents in a mailing envelope:

Completed, signed, and dated forms

Your new passport photo

Your old passport book and/or passport card

Your certified court order

A check for the required amount, made payable to the U.S. Department of State. [See all current Passport fees.](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/passports/how-apply/fees.html)

If you want routine service and live in **California**, **Florida**, **Illinois**, **Minnesota**, **New York**, or **Texas**, mail to:

National Passport Processing Center
Post Office Box 640155
Irving, TX 75064-0155

If you want routine service and live in **any other state **or **Canada**, mail to:

National Passport Processing Center
Post Office Box 90155
Philadelphia, PA 19190-0155

If you want **expedited service**, mail to:

National Passport Processing Center
Post Office Box 90955
Philadelphia, PA 19190-0955
Write “**EXPEDITE**” on the outside of the mailing envelope.

## Wait for your new passport

You should receive your new documents in the mail in 4–6 weeks if you did not pay for expedited service.
