## Get prepared

You need a **completed court order** before changing your Social Security card. There is no cost to change your Social Security details or get a new card.

<details>
<summary>Can I update my gender marker with Social Security?</summary>

Although a gender marker is not displayed on your Social Security card, the Social Security Administration does keep a gender marker on file for each person. However, **we do not recommend attempting to change your gender marker with Social Security at this time.**

From [Lambda Legal](https://lambdalegal.org/tgnc-checklist-under-trump/):

"As of January 31, 2025, the Trump administration has directed the Social Security Administration to stop processing gender marker updates associated with social security records. If you apply for a gender marker change with Social Security now, you will likely be told that they cannot process your request."

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does my sex designation with SSA affect my Social Security benefits?</summary>

No. Social Security benefits do not depend on your listed sex.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Will my Social Security number change?</summary>

No, your number will not change.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What if I’m not a citizen?</summary>

For naturalized citizens or non-citizens, you will need to provide proof of US Citizenship or current work-authorized immigration status.

</details>

## Make an appointment

You may be able to start the process online. The Social Security Administration asks a few short questions to determine how you’ll file. Answer the questions and set up an appointment if necessary.

[Make an appointment](https://www.ssa.gov/personal-record/change-name)

## Await new card

Most people receive their new Social Security card in the mail within 2–4 weeks.
