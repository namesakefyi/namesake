## Get prepared

To update your ID, you will need a completed **court order **certifying your name change. You will also need a **social security card **reflecting your new name.

## Fill out forms

Fill out required documents for the RMV.

[Complete application](https://www.mass.gov/doc/license-and-id-application-0/download)

<details>
<summary>Can I update my ID or license online?</summary>

No. The RMV requires an in-person appointment for name changes.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Do I need a doctor’s note to change my gender marker?</summary>

Nope. You do not need a doctor's note to update your gender marker. Self-select what feels best/safest for you. Massachusetts allows selecting “M”, “F”, and “X”.

</details>

## Schedule an appointment

Name change requests must be completed at an RMV Service Center. You must [**schedule an appointment**](https://atlas-myrmv.massdot.state.ma.us/myrmv/?Link=Appointment) to have your photo taken, your signature captured, and get your new driver's license or ID card.

[Schedule an appointment](https://atlas-myrmv.massdot.state.ma.us/myrmv/?Link=Appointment)

## Show up to your appointment

<Costs costs={[{"title":"Massachusetts ID Card Fee","amount":25,"required":"required"}]} />

Bring your completed application to your appointment. You'll need to bring your proof of name change as well as your new social security card. You may also need proof of residency, such as a recent bill or lease.

The RMV will take your photo and get your new signature.

<details>
<summary>What if I’m applying for a standard driver’s license or ID card?</summary>

If you are applying for a standard driver's license or ID card (without REAL ID), you may change your name with no documentation as long as there is no attempt to defraud.

If you are applying for a REAL ID card, you will need to submit a new photo and signature, and bring your court order and existing ID.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Will I receive a temporary license?</summary>

Yes, the RMV will print a temporary license for you to use while you wait for your official one to arrive. If nothing has arrived after 30 days, call the RMV.

</details>

## Receive your new license or ID

The RMV will mail your replacement driver's license to you with 10–14 days. If you have not received your credential after 30 days, contact the RMV.
