If you're on this page, you've likely already decided on a name. If you haven't, that's okay—go at your own pace!

Some tips for exploration:

- **Browse a name database.** [Behind The Name](https://www.behindthename.com/) allows filtering first names by gender, usage, mythology, and other keywords. They also have a fun [random name generator](https://www.behindthename.com/random/) which can help when exploring ideas.
- **Explore family history.** What were the names of your ancestors? What did they do? Sites like [Ancestry](https://www.ancestry.com) can help with this, or if you have a knowledgable relative, you can strike up a conversation with them about names that have been in your family.
- **Try out names online.** Reddit's [r/TransNames](https://www.reddit.com/r/transnames/about/) is a supportive space for transgender and gender non-conforming individuals to experiment with different names. You can also try on names in the [Namesake Discord](/chat) server.
- **Test the sound of your name in-person.** Waiters and baristas are great options for testing new names. How does the sound of the name feel to hear? Does the other person have questions about pronunciation or spelling?

Finally, it's okay to delay the legal name change process for awhile until you get used to a chosen name. Many trans people wait months, years, or even decades before completing the legal name change process. You can start using a new name with friends without filing any paperwork.

## File a court petition

<Image src={courtPetition} alt="The disembodied arm of the law reaches out from a court building, holding a stack of papers labeled 'Court Order'. In the background, there is a large paper that says 'Name Change Petition'." width={300} densities={[1, 2]} class="float right multiply" />

The first step of the legal name change process to file a *name change petition* and receive a *court order*.

The completed court order is a prerequisite for updating all other legal documents, so you have to do it first.

The petition is just a request to the government saying, "hello, I would like to change my name." A judge will look at your petition and determine whether to approve or deny your request. (Usually they approve it without any issues!)

Each state has a slightly different process for filing the name change petition. Some states require publication in a newspaper beforehand. Some courts may require you to appear in-person, while others don't. Most people can file *pro se* (on their own, without an attorney or legal counsel).

Namesake's [guides](/guides) can help fill out the name change petition and offer advice on filing. Other support can be found via local LGBTQ+ and legal organizations—many of these organizations are listed in our [directory](/directory).

## Update other documents

After you have a certified court order, update your name with Social Security, followed by your State ID or Driver's License, and then your passport.

### Social Security

The process for updating your Social Security card is the same for all states. Follow the [Social Security guide](/guides/social-security) for details.

### State ID or Driver's License

The process to update your State ID or Driver's License varies by state. Follow the state-specific [guide](/guides).

### Passport

The process for updating your U.S. Passport is the same for all states. Follow the [Passport guide](/guides/passport) for details.

### Birth Certificate

You don't need to update your birth certificate unless you want to; most people don't. An updated birth certificate is not a requirement for any other documentation. The process varies by state, and some states do not allow updating birth certificates. Follow the state-specific [guide](/guides).

### Everything else

Once you have the court order and primary identification in your new name, you can change your name almost everywhere else without issue. Some places will even allow for digital updates by scanning in your new ID and court order. What follows is a list of places, in no particular order, where you may want to update your name. Any forms that you fill out should be signed in your new name.

- **Bank:** Bring your new ID and court order and request the name on your account(s) be changed. If you have a debit card or checkbook out of this account, then you will need to request new ones. Any joint account holders must also be present to sign.
- **Credit card:** Most creditors will require you to snail mail or fax a photocopy of your ID and court order. If a particular company is stubborn in updating the account name, consider canceling the card and opening a new card with them or another provider.
- **Work:** If you have an HR system, bring your new ID, new social security card, and court order to them and ask them to update your name. If you get your health insurance through your work, you can have them send the updated information to them on your behalf.
- **Healthcare providers:** You should be able to bring in only your new ID at your next in-person visit and go to the front secretaries to have your name updated in the system. Be aware that certain medical professionals will need to know your transition status.
- **Property/house title:** Contact your local clerk's office and follow their instructions. ID and court order are both likely required.
- **Phone Service:** Go to the nearest store of your carrier network with your new ID and court order and request your information be updated.
- **Taxes:** The Internal Revenue Service (IRS) will be informed of your name change when you update your information with Social Security. You do not need to contact them.
- **Educational records:** Each school will have different requirements and protocols; contact them and see what theirs is.

Some other places to consider:

- Gas/heating provider
- Electricity provider
- Water/sewer company
- Internet service provider
- Garbage service
- Mortgage
- Retirement account
- Clubs/memberships
- Municipal tax authorities
- Online payment services (Venmo, Paypal, etc…)
- Public transit accounts
- Monthly subscriptions (Netflix, Hulu, etc…)
- Voicemail
