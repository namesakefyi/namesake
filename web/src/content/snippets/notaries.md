<details>
  <summary>What is a notary?</summary>

  A notary is someone appointed to witness the signing of important documents,
  like name change petitions. Basically, the notary is making sure that you are
  who you say you are, and that you’re signing the document voluntarily.
</details>

<details>
  <summary>How does the notarization process work?</summary>

  The notary will ask for your unexpired ID with a photograph and signature.
  Your old driver’s license, military ID, green card, or passport should work.
  They’ll review your unsigned court petition and then ask you to sign it.
  They’ll stamp the document, note the signing for their records, and that’s it!
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Where can I find a notary?</summary>

  The most common place to get documents notarized is a local bank branch. There
  may be notary services available at the public library (but call ahead first
  to check). Many UPS stores offer notarization for about $10. There are also
  online notarization services, but these tend to be more expensive than
  in-person (usually starting around $25).

  Additionally, you can [see LGBTQ+ organizations that offer notarization in the Namesake Directory](/directory?service=notary).
</details>
